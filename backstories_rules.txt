BACKSTORIES — COMPLETE GAME RULES & MECHANICS
==============================================
Design by JohnMénard and Anthony Perone
Scenario written by Benoit Bannier and Jeremy Ducret
Published by Cylix Berlin

OVERVIEW
--------
Backstories is a cooperative narrative card game for 1–X players where you complete
objectives and make decisions that shape the story. Most of the time there is no right
or wrong choice — just a different way to play through the story. Every decision has
consequences. The story always progresses even if you fail or make the "wrong" choices.

CONTENTS
--------
• 126 cards total (numbered sequentially)
• 1 rulebook (this document)

IMPORTANT SETUP NOTE
--------------------
Do NOT shuffle or look at the cards before beginning your adventure.
If the cards are out of order for any reason (e.g., you've already played through
the story), sort them back into ascending numerical order before starting.
Make sure all cards face the same way — the backs do NOT have numbers in the top-left
corner; the number appears on the front only.

PLAY SPACE SETUP
----------------
Organize your table into the following areas:

  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  OBJECTIVE AREA │         THE PANORAMA           │    DISCARD    │
  │  (Character +   │   (Situation cards, slots 1–8) │    (pile)     │
  │   Status cards) │                                │               │
  ├─────────────────┴────────────────────────────────┴───────────────┤
  │                     PLAYER AREA (hand)                           │
  │    Action cards, Issue cards, Window/Notch cards                 │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  The DECK lives in its box to one side; draw from it as instructed.

1. Read the first card of the adventure aloud and apply its effects.
2. You can now begin your adventure.

(Tip: The designers suggest leaving the deck in its box and only taking cards out
as needed — this prevents accidentally seeing future cards.)


CARD TYPES (6 types, color-coded)
----------------------------------

1. CHARACTER / OBJECTIVE / ENDING CARDS  [color: red / pink]
   - Tell you what you need to do to continue your adventure (your objectives).
   - Place them in the OBJECTIVE AREA.
   - Allow you to track your hero's status throughout the adventure.
   - ENDING cards are also this type — when drawn, they conclude the story.
   - Note: Slide any Status cards you receive BENEATH this card (red section visible).

2. ISSUE CARDS  [color: orange / amber]
   - Important adventure notes (images or text).
   - Allow you to resolve certain situations or better understand the challenges ahead.
   - Place them in the PLAYER AREA.

3. STORY CARDS  [color: blue / purple]
   - Progress your adventure based on the actions you perform.
   - READ aloud to all players when drawn.
   - Place them in the DISCARD AREA after reading, UNLESS the card tells you otherwise.

4. SITUATION CARDS  [color: brown / tan]
   - Images of locations, people, or items where you can perform actions.
   - Place them in the PANORAMA AREA (they build up a bigger view of the scene).
   - Stay in the Panorama until you are told to discard them.
   - Return a Situation card to the Panorama after using it (unless told to discard it).

5. ACTION CARDS — two sub-types  [color: green / cyan]

   a) WINDOW ACTION CARDS
      - Have a cut-out "window" shape in the center.
      - When played: hold the Window card in front of a Situation card and line up
        the windows. Read the text visible through the window and apply any effects.
      - Start in the PLAYER AREA.

   b) NOTCH ACTION CARDS
      - Have a notch cut into the side of the card (unlike Window cards).
      - When played: hold the Notch card against the side of a Situation card.
        The notch reveals a number on the Situation card.
        Draw that numbered card from the deck.
        If there is no number (just an icon), apply the icon's effect.
        If there is no icon at all, nothing happens.
      - Start in the PLAYER AREA.

6. STATUS CARDS  [color: dark purple / navy / indigo]
   - Placed BENEATH your Character card, keeping the red section visible at the top.
   - NOT discarded — they remain until the END of the adventure.
   - Once placed beneath the Character card, follow the card's further instructions
     (usually: "Turn over this card" to reveal a condition or ongoing effect).
   - A card may reference a Status card (indicated by an icon next to its number).
   - If told to draw a Status card you already have, follow that card's instructions.


PROGRESSING THE STORY
---------------------
Perform actions based on the Action cards available to you. This advances the story.

IMPORTANT: You cannot start a new action until you have completely finished
the previous action (resolved all effects, placed all cards, etc.).

GROUP PLAY
----------
Work together as a team to complete objectives. Set the playing area in the center
of the table so everyone can reach it.

Structured-turn variant:
  Take turns performing actions. You may discuss options as a group, but the
  player whose turn it is makes the final decision. After finishing their action,
  play passes to the left.


HOW TO PLAY AN ACTION
---------------------
Step 1: Choose one Action card from your PLAYER AREA.
Step 2: Choose one Situation card from the PANORAMA AREA.
Step 3: Pick up BOTH cards.
Step 4: Hold the Action card in front of / on top of the Situation card.

  • If it is a WINDOW Action card:
    Line up the windows together.
    Read the text visible in the window.
    Apply any effects listed.

  • If it is a NOTCH Action card:
    Line the notch against the edge of the Situation card.
    The notch reveals a number.
    Draw the numbered card from the deck.
    If there is an icon instead of a number, apply the icon's effect.
    If there is nothing, nothing happens.

Step 5: Return both the Action card and the Situation card to their respective areas
        (Player Area and Panorama), UNLESS an effect tells you to discard them.


APPLYING AN EFFECT
------------------
After using a Window or Notch card, a colored text box displays the effect.
These help progress your adventure and provide clues.

Common effect types:

  [DRAW CARD ICON]
    Draw the numbered card and place it in the indicated position.
    If the card is no longer in the deck, nothing happens — proceed normally.

  [TURN OVER ICON]
    Turn over the card with this icon and read it aloud.

  [DECISION ICON]
    Some effects depend on a decision you make.

  [CONDITION ICON]
    Some effects have a condition — you may only apply the effect if the
    condition is currently true.

MULTIPLE EFFECTS — ORDER MATTERS:
  If a card has multiple effects, apply them in the ORDER you read them.
  Do not skip ahead. Each effect should influence the next before you apply it.
  After applying all effects from one card, read any NEW cards you were told to draw
  and apply their effects in turn.


DISCARDING THE CARD NUMBER (THE MAGNIFYING GLASS RULE)
-------------------------------------------------------
You may sometimes have the opportunity to draw a specific card by solving a riddle or
discovering a secret code (e.g., using a magnifying glass in the story).

You can ONLY draw a specific card from the deck by its number if there is a
MAGNIFYING GLASS icon next to that number.

Without a magnifying glass icon, you cannot freely draw numbered cards — the number
is for the system's internal use only.


END OF THE STORY
----------------
The end of your adventure occurs when you draw an ENDING card.
There are multiple possible endings to each scenario, depending on the choices
you made throughout the adventure.


SPECIAL RULES FOR THE STATUS CARD SCENARIO
-------------------------------------------
In scenarios that use Status cards:
  • Status cards are NEVER discarded during the adventure.
  • Their consequences persist until the very end of the adventure.
  • A card's effect may reference a Status card by icon (shown next to the card number).
  • If you are told to draw a Status card you already have, follow the instructions
    printed on that Status card.
  • Status cards are slid BENEATH the Character card, with only the red top section
    visible. The card will give further instructions (usually to turn it over).


ZONE REFERENCE SUMMARY
-----------------------
DECK          — Cards not yet in play. Sorted numerically in the box.
PLAYER AREA   — Your hand: holds Action (Window/Notch) cards and Issue cards.
PANORAMA      — The scene board: holds Situation cards in up to 8 numbered slots.
OBJECTIVE     — Holds your Character card + any Status cards beneath it. Also holds
                Ending cards when drawn.
DISCARD       — Used/read cards. Story cards always go here after being read.
                Some Situation/Action cards are discarded per effect instructions.
STORY (read)  — Optional staging zone: place a Story card here while reading it
                aloud to your group before moving it to Discard.


CARD TYPE → DEFAULT ZONE MAPPING
----------------------------------
CHARACTER     → OBJECTIVE AREA
ENDING        → OBJECTIVE AREA (concludes the game)
ISSUE         → PLAYER AREA
STORY         → DISCARD (after reading aloud)
SITUATION     → PANORAMA AREA
ACTION_WINDOW → PLAYER AREA
NOTCH         → PLAYER AREA
STATUS        → OBJECTIVE AREA (beneath CHARACTER card)


INTERACTION MATRIX (Action × Situation)
-----------------------------------------
Each Action card × Situation card combination may have a defined result stored in
the game's interaction table. Possible result_types:

  DRAW_CARD          — Draw a specific card number from the deck.
  DRAW_STORY         — Draw a Story card and read it aloud.
  DRAW_STATUS        — Place a Status card beneath the Character card.
  DISCARD_SITUATION  — Remove the Situation card from the Panorama.
  NOTHING            — No effect (the notch/window has no match here).
  CUSTOM             — A narrative text effect printed directly on the card.


DIGITAL ENGINE — ADDITIONAL NOTES
-----------------------------------
In the digital version:

• DRAG an Action card onto a Situation card in the Panorama to perform an action.
• The "Draw by Number" input lets you draw a specific card if it has a
  magnifying-glass icon (has_magnifying_glass = true in the database).
• STATUS cards dragged to the Objective Area are automatically stacked beneath
  the Character card.
• Story cards dragged to the Story reading zone are displayed for the group to read
  before being discarded.
• Real-time sync via Supabase Realtime ensures all players see card movements
  immediately.
• Voice chat via LiveKit lets players communicate without leaving the game.
