# Back Stories — Physical Card Catalog
## Game: **Alone Under the Ice** (confirmed — card 1 back face; written by Benoit Bannier & Jérémy Ducret; art by Cyrille Bertin)

This catalog documents all physical card examples photographed for the Back Stories board game. It is the primary design reference for The Icarus-7 Paradox digital card set.

---

## DESIGN SYSTEM OVERVIEW

### Card Color / Brand System (by card type)

| Card Type | Corner Spool Color | Background / Theme |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| ACTION / WINDOW | Blue spool (cobalt) | Light blue-grey gradient, mountain silhouette at bottom, geometric triangle motif |
| NOTCH | Blue spool (cobalt) | Same as Window but with right-edge semicircular notch cutout |
| STORY | Yellow/gold spool | Warm tan/cream gradient, mountain silhouette |
| SITUATION (Panorama) | Blue spool (dark navy) | Illustrated scene fills full card face; minimap grid in top center; caption strip at bottom |
| OBJECTIVE / LOCATION | Purple spool | Purple header band, art below, OBJECTIVE text in banner at bottom |
| STATUS | Red spool (crimson) | Red header band across top with status name; character illustration below |
| CLUE (Item) | Green spool | Light grey-green gradient, item illustration center, caption at bottom |

### Universal Card Anatomy

**Front face:**
- Top-left corner: spool/rope icon with card number in white text
- Top-center (Situation cards only): 2×2 minimap grid (grey squares, one red = current position)
- Top-right (Situation cards only): sometimes a red "Discard X" pill
- Main area: illustration or text
- Bottom strip: short italic caption on a translucent "scroll" label, with a compass/clock icon at right

**Back face (Story/Action cards):**
- Card number repeated in top-left margin and bottom-right margin (for multi-player use at a table — multiple players can read simultaneously)
- White text on mountain-gradient background
- Action buttons styled as colored "pill" badges with icons:
  - GREEN pill + hand icon = "Draw [N]" — draw a specific card
  - RED pill + X icon = "Discard [N]" or "Discard this card"
  - BLUE pill + arrow icon = "Turn over this card" or "Turn over [Card name]"
  - YELLOW/GOLD pill + left-arrow icon = "Return this card to the deck"
  - ORANGE warning band (triangle icon) = conditional rule / prerequisite check
- STATUS gate: orange warning band shows emoji icon for status type

### The Window Mechanic (Physical)
Window cards have a **real rectangular die-cut hole** punched through the card (approx. 60% card width, 20% card height). The window is positioned either at the TOP third or BOTTOM third of the card depending on the card number. When laid over a Situation card, the window frames specific text or image content on the card beneath. The action label (e.g. "Search / Gather / Explore the area") appears below or above the window on the same card face.

### The Notch Mechanic (Physical)
Notch cards have a **semicircular cutout** on one edge — observed on right edge (card 6 John's letter, card 42 Padlock key, card 63 Vial) and left edge (card 44 Signet ring, card 69 Chain). The notch physically aligns with the edge of a Situation card when placed beside it, forming a tactile connection point. Card title uses bold blue display font with a triangular "pennant" shape behind it.

### The Magnifying Glass Marker
Some Story/Action cards (e.g. card 30) show a magnifying glass (🔍) icon next to the card number. This marks cards that contain a hidden code or number the player must find and use elsewhere.

### Card 1
- **Type**: STORY (Intro/Scene-setter) — **THE GAME'S OPENING CARD**
- **Spool**: Gold/yellow (Story type)
- **Front**: Full illustrated cover — the cable-car station of the Nid d'Aigle refuge perched on snowy alpine peaks under a dramatic purple-grey sky. Bold orange text at bottom: **"Location 1 / The Nid d'Aigle Refuge"**. Body text (verbatim):
  > "It's been almost two weeks since John, your brother, made contact with you. The last you heard from him was the letter he sent from Mont Blanc. Having not heard any more news, you decide to track him down. It's unlike him, especially as he hasn't replied and no one knows where he is. Determined to find him, you trace his steps, which lead you to the Nid d'Aigle refuge."
  > "Night is drawing in as you reach the refuge. You see several people going about their business. Leaving your belongings at the entrance, you take a look around the place."
- **Action buttons**: GREEN "Draw 2 to 10" | BLUE "Turn over this card"
- **Back**: **Scenario card — ALWAYS STAYS ON TOP OF DECK.** Title: "ALONE UNDER THE ICE". Subtitle: "Scenario written by Benoit BANNIER and Jérémy DUCRET / Artwork by Cyrille BERTIN". Art: silhouette of a lone figure standing inside a glowing blue ice cave tunnel. Orange warning band at bottom: "Keep this card on the top of the deck at all times."
- **Notes**: This IS the scenario's title card. The scenario is confirmed as "Alone Under the Ice." Card 1 is a two-sided card — front is the opening narrative, back is the scenario identity card. The scenario designer credits are Benoit Bannier & Jérémy Ducret; artist Cyrille Bertin.

## KEY MECHANICAL PATTERNS FOR ICARUS-7 DIGITAL DESIGN

### 1. The Two-Stage Status System
Every STATUS card has a front face (first injury) and a back face (escalated injury). The convention:
- Front: "IF TOLD TO DRAW [N] AGAIN → Turn over this card"
- Back: "IF TOLD TO DRAW [N] AGAIN → Turn over your Character" (= death or major consequence)
- Card numbering: Back is "N*" (asterisk = escalated)

**Icarus-7 equivalents**: FROZEN/HYPOTHERMIA → STATUS: PARANOID → STATUS: LUCID. The escalation pattern should be preserved.

### 2. The Panorama Update System
Situation cards can be REPLACED in the grid by newer Situation cards:
- Replacement triggers are shown as red "Discard X" pills in the TOP-RIGHT of the NEW card's front face
- The old card is discarded; new card takes same grid slot (same minimap position)
- This creates a "living board" that reflects narrative state

### 3. Status Gates on Story Cards
Status-gated cards use a consistent ORANGE WARNING BAND with triangle icon at the top of the card back. The pattern:
- "If you have the [ICON] or [ICON] card: Turn over this card without reading on."
- The "without reading on" instruction means the player sees only the back face (which was already read before the gate check) — creating different outcomes for the same card.

### 4. Multi-Player Text Duplication
Every Story card back contains 4 identical (or nearly identical) copies of the text, arranged in quadrants. This allows all players at the table to read simultaneously. Card number appears in top-left and bottom-right margins of each quadrant.

### 5. Window Positioning Is Meaningful
Window cards have the cutout at different vertical positions:
- TOP window (cards 3, 51): reveals upper portion of Situation card (title area, header info)
- MIDDLE window (cards 4, 52): reveals middle content (main illustration)
- BOTTOM window (cards 5, 12, 17, 57-59, 65, 94): reveals lower portion (caption strip, specific detail)

In the digital implementation, the window overlay should animate the specific window position to frame the matching area of the Situation card.

### 6. Notch Positioning Is Also Meaningful
Notch cards have the cutout at different edge positions:
- RIGHT edge (cards 6, 27): used with Situation cards to the right
- LEFT edge (cards 44, 63): used with Situation cards to the left
- BOTTOM corners (card 42): dual-notch for unlocking (key metaphor)
- BOTH edges (cards 14, 19 — clothing): wearable item slot format

### 7. The Compass/Clock Icon
Every Situation card front has a small compass or clock-face icon at the far right of the caption strip. This is purely decorative/thematic — it does not encode game information. Include in digital Situation card design for authenticity.

### 8. Card Roping/Spool Icon Colors Map to Card Types
| Spool Color | Card Type |
|-------------|-----------|
| Dark cobalt blue | ACTION (Window, Notch), CLUE/ITEM |
| Gold/yellow | STORY (narrative read-aloud) |
| Dark navy | SITUATION (Panorama) |
| Purple | OBJECTIVE / LOCATION |
| Red/crimson | STATUS (health/condition) |
| Green | CLUE (document, evidence — notebook) |

### 9. NPC Dialogue is In-Character Voice
NPC dialogue appears verbatim in Story card text. The manager (warm, helpful, ultimately firm) is the primary NPC analog. Her dialogue style:
- Helpful but limits information: "I haven't met him but maybe you could ask the manager?"
- Escalating patience: eventually ejects player if too aggressive
- Rewards honest social play: gives key (card 42) freely if asked properly

**Direct analog for V.I.C.T.O.R.**: The manager's warmth masking limits is the exact tone profile. She genuinely wants to help but will not tolerate rule-breaking. V.I.C.T.O.R. should sound like this but with the warmth slightly off — too reassuring, slightly pre-scripted.

### 10. Consumable Items Discard When Used
Item cards with "Discard this card once used" (matches 57/58/59) or "You may discard this card at any time to [effect]" (coffee card 49) establish the consumable mechanic. These items have a special capsule-shaped bottom label in the Situation card caption style.
